# Background & Objective

## The Business

Hudson's Dry Cleaning (Master Dry Cleaners) operates **six locations** across Melbourne:

| Location | Type |
| --- | --- |
| **Albert Park** | Retail (Service, Delivery) |
| **Seddon** | Retail (Service, Alterations) |
| **Werribee** | Retail (Shop Front, Delivery) |
| **Dr Drapes** | Specialist (Production, Installation, Admin) |
| **The Butler** | Retail (Customer Service) |
| **Laundry** | Central production facility (10+ departments) |

The business employs approximately **60 staff** across casual, full-time, and salaried roles on a **fortnightly pay cycle** through KeyPay.

---

## The Problem

Hudson's currently faces challenges in **controlling wage costs relative to revenue**, particularly as sales fluctuate week to week:

- **Rosters are created with expected shift costs**, but actual timesheets frequently diverge — extra hours, unrostered shifts, missed breaks, and overtime push costs above budget
- **There is no automated visibility** into where roster and timesheets differ, by how much, or why
- **Wage percentage blowouts** occur without early warning — by the time payroll runs, the money is already spent
- **Managers lack the data** to have informed conversations with staff about roster adherence

---

## The Objective

Build an **ongoing monitoring tool** that compares rostered (planned) labour against actual (timesheet/payslip) labour to:

1. **Identify variances** between what was rostered and what was actually worked
2. **Quantify the cost impact** of those variances in dollars
3. **Flag specific employees and locations** where rosters aren't being followed
4. **Track wage cost as a percentage of revenue** over time
5. **Give management actionable data** to drive roster adherence and control labour costs
